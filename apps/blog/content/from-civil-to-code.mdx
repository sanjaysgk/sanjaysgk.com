In 2016, I was a civil engineering student in Bangalore. My tools were AutoCAD, STAAD Pro, and Civil 3D. By 2024, I was at Monash University, writing Python pipelines to predict which mutant peptides might help the immune system fight cancer.

## The Spark

It started with a hackathon. Someone showed me you could build a working app in 48 hours. I was hooked — not because the app was good, but because the feedback loop was instant. In civil engineering, you wait months to see if your design works. In code, you press run and find out in seconds.

## Self-Teaching (2017-2020)

I taught myself Python while still studying civil engineering. Then came 50+ online courses on Coursera: Deep Learning Specialisation, Big Data, MLOps.

The pivotal moment was realising I didn't want to optimise concrete mix designs — I wanted to optimise algorithms. The analytical thinking from engineering transferred perfectly. Structural analysis is just linear algebra with gravity.

## Monash (2022-2024)

Moving to Melbourne for a Master of Data Science at Monash was the leap of faith. But the real discovery was finding bioinformatics — the intersection of biology and computing.

Everything clicked. The engineering discipline. The coding skills. The data science training. All converging on one question: can we use computation to understand disease?

## Advice for Career Switchers

- **Your previous skills aren't wasted** — they're your edge
- **Self-teaching is real** — but formalise it eventually
- **Follow the energy** — I didn't plan to end up in cancer research. I followed what made me stay up at night building things.
